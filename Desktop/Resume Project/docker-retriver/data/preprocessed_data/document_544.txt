Article 178: The Speaker and Deputy Speaker of the Legislative Assembly - Constitution of India Part VI Article 178 The Speaker and Deputy Speaker of the Legislative Assembly Every Legislative Assembly of a State shall, as soon as may be, choose two members of the Assembly to be respectively Speaker and Deputy Speaker thereof and, so often as the office of Speaker or Deputy Speaker becomes vacant, the Assembly shall choose another member to be Speaker or Deputy Speaker, as the case may be. Version 1 Article 157, Draft Constitution of India 1948 Every Legislative Assembly of a State shall, as soon as may be, choose two members of the Assembly to be respectively Speaker and Deputy Speaker thereof, and, so often as the office of Speaker or Deputy Speaker becomes vacant, the Assembly shall choose another member to be Speaker or Deputy Speaker, as the case may be. Version 2 Article 178, Constitution of India 1950 Every Legislative Assembly of a State shall, as soon as may be, choose two members of the Assembly to be respectively Speaker and Deputy Speaker thereof and, so often as the office of Speaker or Deputy Speaker becomes vacant, the Assembly shall choose another member to be Speaker or Deputy Speaker, as the case may be. Summary Draft Article 157 (Article 178) was debated on 2 June 1949. It established the offices of Speaker and Deputy Speaker in State Legislative Assemblies. The Draft Article was adopted without debate on 2 June 1949.
